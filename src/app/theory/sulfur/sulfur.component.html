Lead Acetate Test for Sulfur

The Lead Acetate test is a test used to detect the presence of sulfur in an organic compound. In this test, a compound is then mixed with hydrogen peroxide and bring to a boil and then added with the Lead Acetate solution. The presence of a dark brown or black precipitate indicates the presence of sulfur in the compound.

Objective

The objective for this experiment is to detect the presence of Sulfur by the use of different compounds.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Students acquire the skill in understanding the Lead Acetate Test for Sulfur detection.
  2. Students understand the test to detect the presence of Sulfur in Albumin, NaOH, Lead Acetate compound.
  3. Students understand the chemical reactions, distinguish positive result.
  4. Students acquire the skill to perform the experiment in the online lab simulation once they understand different steps in the procedure.
