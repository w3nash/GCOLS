Soda Lime Test for Nitrogen

The soda lime test is a chemical test used to identify and detect the presence of carboxylic acids and nitrogen in an organic compound. In this test, a small amount of compound is heated with soda lime. The liberation of a foul-smelling ammonia gas indicates the presence of nitrogen compounds in the solution. But this test is not applicable to compounds that have a -NO2 and diazo derivatives as they do not respond to this test hence having a negative result, do not indicate the lack in the presence of nitrogen.

Objective

The objective for this experiment is to detect the presence of Nitrogen by the use of different compounds.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Students acquire the skill in understanding the Soda Lime Test for Nitrogen detection.
  2. Students understand the test to detect the presence of Nitrogen in Soda Lime and Urea.
  3. Students understand the chemical reactions, distinguish positive and negative result.
  4. Students acquire the skill to perform the experiment in the online lab simulation once they understand different steps in the procedure.
