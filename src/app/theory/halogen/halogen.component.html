Beilstein Test for Halogen

The Beilstein test is a test developed by Friedrich Konrad Beilstein that is used to detect the presence of halogens in a solution, although it does not distinguish between chlorine, bromine, or iodine. In this test. A copper wire is dipped into the halogen-containing solution and thrust into a flame. The copper oxide on the wire reacts with the organic halide to produce a copper-halide compound that gives a blue-green color to the flame.

Objective

The objective for this experiment is to detect the presence of Halogen by the use of different compounds.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Students acquire the skill in understanding the Beilstein Test for Halogen detection.
  2. Students understand the test to detect the presence of Halogen in Chlorine compound.
  3. Students understand the chemical reactions, distinguish positive and negative result.
  4. Students acquire the skill to perform the experiment in the online lab simulation once they understand different steps in the procedure.
