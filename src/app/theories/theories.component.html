Detection of Elements Theories

An Organic Compound is defined as a chemical compound that is composed of Carbon and Hydrogen in its nature. But there are a few exceptions like CCl4 and CS2 etc. and other elements can exist alongside hydrogen and carbon in an organic compound; and they are the nitrogen, oxygen, sulfur and halogens. The prominence of the said additional elements are being subjected in the identification and classification of different organic compounds. The Detection of these common elements in an organic compound is detected with the use of different tests and methods used to identify the specific element present in a compound.

Ferrox Paper Test for Oxygen


Soda Lime Test for Nitrogen


Beilstein Test for Halogen


Lead Acetate Test for Sulfur
